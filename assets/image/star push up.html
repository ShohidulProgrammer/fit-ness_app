How To Do Push-Ups

Love them or loathe them, push-ups are a foundational part of many HIIT workouts.

Share

Push-ups are a fast and effective way of building upper-body strength, but only if your technique is on point!

The Right Technique

To make sure your form is correct, run through these 5 steps every time you perform a push-up:

  1. Get onto all fours, with your hands placed wider than your shoulders.
  2. Extend your arms and legs to reach the top of your push-up.
  3. With your back straight, engage your abs to support your core.
  4. Keep your neck straight, facing the ground, and lower your body slowly.
  5. Pause briefly at the bottom, then push yourself back up.

Beginner Variations

If full push-ups aren't part of your fitness routine yet, knee push-ups are a great place to start. They have many of the same benefits as full push-ups and can help you build the strength you need for the full exercise.

All you have to do is start in a regular push-up position and then slowly drop your knees, instead of keeping them extended. If you aren't able to fully lower down to the ground, try only lowering your chest to the height of your elbow height - and remember to place a mat under your knees if it's uncomfortable!

Wall Push-Ups

Another beginner option is the Wall Push-Up. Wall Push-Ups are a perfect opportunity to learn the movement while protecting sore joints and preventing injuries. They're also a great way to build strength after injury, or if you've had an extended break from upper-body workouts.

Perform wall push-ups by standing around 2 feet from the wall, with your palms on the wall about shoulder-width apart. Bend your arms and lower towards the wall with a straight back until your nose almost touches it, then push back and repeat.

Advanced Variations

If push-ups are a regular part of your fitness routine, you can gain even more muscle-building benefits by adding variations! To begin with, experiment with moving your hands closer together or further apart, to test placing the load on different parts of the upper body.

A narrow grip will target the arms and chest, while a wider grip will be more taxing on the muscles of the chest and shoulders. Try Diamond Push-Ups and Star Push-Ups in Seven for the full effect!
